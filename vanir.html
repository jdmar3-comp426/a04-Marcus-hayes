The Vanir

The Vanir were one of the two primary tribes of deities in Pre-Christian Norse mythology. The Vanir consisted of the minor Norse gods who resided in Vanaheim (one of the nine worlds within the branches of the world-tree, Yggdrasil) and possessed connections to the earthly plane. The Vanir included Freya, Freyr, and Njord. Very little is known about the Vanir, as the Aesir received most of the recognition and appreciation among Norse followers.
