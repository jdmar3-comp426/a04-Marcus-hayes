Yggdrasil, the World-Tree

Yggdrasil, the World Tree, is the gargantuan tree upon whose branches and roots the Nine Worlds of Nordic mythology rest upon, and exists as the center and connection between the entire cosmos. The name Yggdrasil translates to "Horse of Odin", referencing an instance when Odin sacrificed himself on the tree by hanging himself, stabbed himself with a spear, and hung for days without help, food, and water: all done in order to gain the power and knowledge of runes. The sight was said the resemble a horse and rider. Lore suggested that there would come a time when Yggdrasil would tremble, and this would indicate the beginning of Ragnarok, a cataclysmic event that would destroy the universe.
